Bomb blast diplomacy

It is hard to be on good terms with the neightbours when they keep blowing up your house.
Yet, since 2003, India has sought to make friends with Pakistan while continuing to accuse it of abetting hedeous bomb attacks in its cities.
Pakistan brushes these accusations aside, while at the same time promising not to allow it soil to be used as a base for terrorism.
This attitude makes the bombings seem an insuperable obstacle to peace.
They may yet prove so.
But they have at least become central to a peae process often marked by bickering over more trivial matters.
