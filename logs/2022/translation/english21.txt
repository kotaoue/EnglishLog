Firefighters lack on-site experience.

To help firefighters conduct drills in realstic settings, the Fire and Disaster Management Agency (FDMA) has decided to promote from this fiscal year the introduction of training facilities that simulate the inside of a burning building.

The annual number of building fires has halved over the last four decades, resulting in a growing number of firefighters who have little experience in battling real blazes.
About 20 percent of national taxes allocated to local governments will be used for prefectural governments to prepare training facilities.

The facilities will include so-called "hot fire traininig facilities" that re-create a fire in a building by burning wood pellets.
They give firefighters the opportunity to experience dangerous situations they need to be aware of, such as when water is discharged over smoke clusterd near the ceiling of a room and the smoke dispersers, suddenly bluring their version.

On April 3, the FDMA sent a notice to prefectural governments urging them to set up such facilities.
