The Surprising Brexit Vote

On June 24, 2016, British citizens woke up to startling news:
In an election held the day before, the public had unexpectedly voted to formally withdraw tfrom the European Union.
The results were close, but decisive:
Only 48.1% had voted to remain in the EU, while 51.89% were against staying.
Almost no one had predicted this outcome.
Just before the election, poll averates showed that approximately 51% of the population were in favor of keeping EU membership.
What went wrong?
For one, experts pointed out that the 3-point discrepancy was still within the margin of error for most polls.
They also noted that polls depend on honesty.
Even in anonymous polls, people sometimes conceal their real opinions.
This is especially true when they support an unpopular position.
