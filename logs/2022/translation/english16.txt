Exercise Enhances Learning in Children

"Sound body, sound mind" has long been accepted wisdom.
Now it seems that research actually backs this up.
Researchers from the University of Illinois suggest that doing moderate exercise in the classroom helps school children stay alert and focused.
After 20 minutes of in-class exercise, students perfomed 10 percent better on problem-solving tasks.

Schools that offer "action-based learning" are getting spectacular results.
At Naperville Cnetral Hight School near Chicago, kids who struggle with math and are poor in reading are constantly on the move in their classes.
They use exercise bikes and treadmills right in the classroom while reading assignments or reciting multiplication tables.
At Naperville the reading scores have nearly doubled, and the math scores have surged by a factor of 20.
