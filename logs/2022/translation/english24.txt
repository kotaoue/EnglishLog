Interracial Marriages Increasing in the U.S.

For years interracial marriage in the U.S. was frowned upon.
It was forbidden in many states, but in 1967 the Supreme Court ruled that marriage across racial lines was legal throughout the country.
For the last 50 years, the number of interracial marriages has been steadily increasing.
Today about 11 million people in the U.S. have a spouse of a different race or ethnicity.

One factor contributing to this increase is a gradual shift in attitudes.
In a rcent Pew Research Center survey released in 2017, 39% of Americans say interracial marriage is generally good for society, up from 24% in 2010.
Also, many indicate that they are less likely to disapprove of as close relative marrying someone of a different race or ethnicity.
More specifically, 90% wouldn't oppose such a marriage, up from 69% in 2010.
