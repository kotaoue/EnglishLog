U.N vote to add 15 North Koreans, four entities to U.N. blacklist

UNITED NATIONS/TOKYO - The U.N. Security Council voted unanimously Friday to add 15 individuals and four entities linked to North Korea's nuclear and missile programs to a U.N. sanctions blacklist, but Chinese opposition bloced touch new sanctions pushed by the United States.

While the resolution will impose a global travel ban and asset freeze on a range of North Koreans including the man believed to head its overseas espionage operations and foreign intelligence collection, it will not target critical oil deliveries or include other tough sanctions.

The U.S. and its allies strongly disagreed with China and Russia on how to rein in North Korea's rapidly escalating nuclear and ballistic missle program, which Pyongyang says is aimed at developing a long-range missile capable of reaching the United States with as nuclear warhead.
