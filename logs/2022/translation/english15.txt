Philosophy Taught to Yount Children

Eight-year-olds engage in philosophical debate several times a month at their elementary school in Springfield, Masachusetts.
The children are taught by undergraduate philosophy students from Nount Holyoke College, who user classic children's books to rise basic questions about life.
The youngsters then convey their views about these issues quite vigorously to one another.

For years, many have believed the ability to think abstractly does not occur in early childhood.
In his widely accepted theories of child development, Swiss psychologist Jean Piaget asserted that children develop this capacity no earlier than the age of 12.

However, recently some philosophers have found that younger children exhibit the curiosity and sense of wonder that is needed to deal with such philosohical issues.
