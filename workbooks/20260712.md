## テーマ: The Impact of Quantum Computing on Modern Cryptography

### 英文パッセージ

Quantum computing represents a fundamental paradigm shift in computational power. Unlike classical computers, which process information in bits as either 0 or 1, quantum computers utilize qubits. These qubits can exist in multiple states simultaneously through a phenomenon known as superposition, allowing them to perform complex calculations in parallel at an exponential scale. While the technology is still in its nascent stages, its potential implications for cybersecurity are profound and, to some, deeply unsettling.

The primary concern lies in the vulnerability of current encryption standards, such as RSA. Most modern digital security relies on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers—a task that would take even the most powerful classical supercomputers thousands of years to complete. However, Shor’s algorithm, a quantum-based mathematical framework, has theoretically proven that a sufficiently powerful quantum computer could factor these numbers almost instantaneously. This would effectively render conventional encryption methods obsolete, exposing everything from personal bank details to classified national security data.

In response to this looming threat, the field of "Post-Quantum Cryptography" (PQC) has emerged. Researchers are now racing to develop cryptographic systems that are resistant to quantum attacks. These new protocols often rely on different mathematical foundations, such as lattice-based cryptography, which are believed to be secure against both classical and quantum computational capabilities. The transition to these standards is not merely a technical necessity but a race against time. There is a growing concern regarding "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks, where sensitive encrypted data is stolen today with the intention of decrypting it once quantum technology matures. Consequently, the push for quantum-resistant infrastructure has become a top priority for governments and global tech corporations alike.
