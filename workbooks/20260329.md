## テーマ: 学生にとっての睡眠の重要性

### 英文パッセージ

Many students often *sacrifice* sleep to study more or to enjoy their free time, but this can actually harm their performance. Getting enough sleep is not just about resting your body; it's crucial for your brain to function at its best. For high school students, a recommended 7-9 hours of sleep per night is essential for both academic success and overall well-being.

When you get enough sleep, your brain can process information more effectively, which improves your concentration in class and your ability to remember what you’ve learned. This means you can understand complex topics faster and recall facts during exams. Furthermore, adequate sleep helps regulate your mood, making you feel more energetic and less stressed. It also strengthens your immune system, helping you stay healthy and avoid missing school due to illness.

On the other hand, lack of sleep can lead to serious problems. You might find it hard to focus, forget things easily, and feel irritable. Long-term sleep deprivation can even weaken your body's defenses, making you more vulnerable to colds and flu. Your academic performance can suffer significantly, as you'll struggle to keep up with assignments and absorb new information.

So, how can you improve your sleep habits? First, try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps set your body's internal clock. Second, avoid screens like phones, tablets, and computers at least an hour before bedtime, as the light they emit can interfere with sleep. Third, make sure your bedroom is dark, quiet, and cool. Although it might seem difficult to manage your time, making sleep a priority will greatly benefit your academic life and overall well-being. Good sleep is truly a foundation for success.
