### 英文パッセージ

Let's talk about plastic. It's an amazing material, used for countless items in our daily lives, from food packaging and water bottles to electronic devices and car parts. However, its widespread use has led to a significant global challenge: plastic pollution. When we throw away plastic products, many of them end up in landfills or, even worse, in our oceans. Scientists estimate that some plastics can take hundreds of years, or even thousands, to fully decompose, causing long-term damage to ecosystems. Marine animals, for instance, often mistake plastic debris for food, leading to severe health problems or death.

To tackle this issue, everyone needs to get involved. One simple yet effective step is to "reduce, reuse, and recycle." Instead of buying single-use plastic bottles, carry a reusable water bottle. When shopping, remember to bring your own cloth bags instead of using plastic ones provided by stores. Many cafes now offer discounts if you bring your own cup for coffee. Furthermore, supporting companies that use sustainable packaging or produce items from recycled materials can also contribute to a greener future. Although these individual actions might seem small, collectively they can create a powerful positive impact on our planet. It is crucial for us to understand that our choices today shape the world of tomorrow.
