## テーマ: The Concept of "Polycrisis" and Systemic Resilience

### 英文パッセージ

In the contemporary era, the global community is increasingly grappling with what scholars describe as a "polycrisis"—a predicament where multiple global emergencies are so deeply interconnected that the whole is more dangerous than the sum of its parts. Unlike a series of isolated challenges, a polycrisis involves the inextricable entwinement of ecological, economic, and geopolitical disruptions. For instance, climate-induced migration can trigger political instability, which in turn disrupts global supply chains, leading to economic volatility that hampers further investment in green technologies.

This systemic entanglement poses a significant challenge to traditional governance models, which are often structured in "silos." Bureaucratic institutions typically specialize in singular domains, such as trade, health, or defense. However, in a polycrisis, a solution in one domain may inadvertently exacerbate a crisis in another. This necessitates a shift toward holistic "systemic thinking," where policymakers move beyond linear problem-solving to embrace complexity and nonlinearity.

Furthermore, the psychological toll of such a landscape cannot be ignored. The pervasive sense of uncertainty can lead to a collective paralysis, where the magnitude of the crises overwhelms the capacity for collective action. To navigate this, leaders must foster "radical resilience"—not merely the ability to bounce back to the status quo, but the capacity to evolve through disruption. The goal is to build adaptive systems that can withstand the cascading failures inherent in a hyper-connected world.
