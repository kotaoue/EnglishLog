## テーマ: The Ethical Implications of Genetic Engineering（遺伝子工学の倫理的影響）

### 英文パッセージ

The advent of CRISPR-Cas9 technology has ushered in a new era of biological manipulation, offering unprecedented precision in editing the genetic code. While the potential to eradicate hereditary diseases such as cystic fibrosis or sickle cell anemia is undeniably profound, the rapid progression of this field has ignited a fierce ethical debate. Critics argue that we are treading on dangerous ground, potentially opening a "Pandora's box" of unintended consequences that could permanently alter the human germline.

One of the most contentious issues is the distinction between therapeutic intervention and human enhancement. While most agree that curing debilitating illnesses is a noble pursuit, the prospect of "designer babies"—where traits like intelligence, physical prowess, or aesthetic features are deliberately selected—raises significant concerns regarding social inequality. If such technologies are accessible only to the affluent, it could lead to the emergence of a biological caste system, further widening the gap between the privileged and the marginalized.

Moreover, the long-term ecological impact of gene drives—technologies designed to propagate specific genes throughout a population—remains largely speculative. Altering one species to eliminate pests or diseases could disrupt entire ecosystems in ways that are currently unpredictable. As the scientific community races forward, the necessity for robust international regulatory frameworks becomes increasingly urgent. We must balance the drive for innovation with a profound sense of responsibility toward future generations and the planet's biodiversity.
